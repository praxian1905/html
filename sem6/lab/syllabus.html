Course Outline

Detailed Syllabus: CAD/CAM

A complete mapping of Computer-Aided Design, Engineering, and Manufacturing concepts structured for advanced academic study.

📚 6 Core Units ⏱️ Full Semester ⚙️ Theory & Application

📋 Table of Contents

  1. Unit 1: Basics of CAD
  2. Unit 2: Curves and Surfaces
  3. Unit 3: Solid Modelling
  4. Unit 4: Computer Aided Engineering
  5. Unit 5: Computer Aided Manufacturing
  6. Unit 6: Latest Technologies
Unit 01

01. Basics of CAD

  • Need and Scope of Computer Aided Design.
  • Fundamentals of CAD and computer graphics.
  • Application Areas, and Comparison of CAD with Manual designing.
  • System Basics: Benefits of CAD, User Coordinate System (UCS), 3D systems.
  • 2D Geometrical Transformations: Matrix representations and homogeneous coordinates.
  • Advanced Transformations: Composite transformations, transformations between coordinate systems.
  • 3D Graphics: 2-D viewing, 3-D Geometric transformations, composite transformations.
  • Importance of transformations in spatial representation.
Unit 02

02. Curves and Surfaces

ANALYTIC CURVES

Basic Geometries

Mathematical representation of simple entities: Circle, Arc, Ellipse, Parabola, and Hyperbola.

SYNTHETIC CURVES

Splines & Continuity

Concept of continuity (C0, C1, C2). Cubic Splines: equations, properties, and blending functions.

ADVANCED CURVES

Bezier & B-Splines

Bezier Curve equations and properties. Introduction to B-Splines and the extensive advantages of NURBS.

🚗 Automotive Application

Detailed study of various types of surfaces (ruled, swept, lofted) used specifically in the automotive industry alongside their practical applications.

Unit 03

03. Solid Modelling

  • Geometry and Topology: Differentiating the mathematical shape from the structural connectivity.
  • Modelling Paradigms: Comparison between wireframe, surface, and solid models.
  • Core Properties: Properties of solid models and their representation schemes.
  • Mathematical Foundations: Concept of Half-spaces and Boolean operations (Union, Intersect, Subtract).

Representation Schemes

Boundary Representation (B-rep) Constructive Solid Geometry (CSG) Sweep Representation Primitive Instancing Cell Decomposition Techniques
Unit 04

04. Computer Aided Engineering (CAE)

This unit dives into the simulation and validation of digital designs before manufacturing.

  • Introduction to simulation, interference detection, and failure analysis under static and dynamic loading.
  • Preprocessing: Elements used in CAE, material defining, meshing, and determining optimal mesh density.
  • Setup: Application of loading and constraints, defining boundary conditions.
  • Solving & Post-processing: Understanding solvers, reviewing the results, and validating CAE outputs.
  • Case Study: Practical application and workflow demonstration using ANSYS software.
Unit 05

05. Computer Aided Manufacturing (CAM)

NC / CNC / DNC

Numerical Control

Introduction, roles, advantages, disadvantages, and applications of NC. Features of CNC systems, DNC networks, adaptive control, and safety measures.

FLEXIBILITY

FMS Components

Flexible Manufacturing Systems (FMS): Equipment, control systems, Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs), and Automated Storage and Retrieval Systems (AS/RS).

INTEGRATION

CIM & CAPP

Principles of Computer Integrated Manufacturing (CIM) and Computer Aided Process Planning (CAPP) spanning variant and generative approaches.

Unit 06

06. Latest Technologies

  • Industry 4.0: Introduction to the interconnected smart factory paradigm.
  • FEM: Advanced Finite Element Method implementations.
  • Robotics & Automation: Industrial robotics, kinematics, and applications.
  • Reverse Engineering: Techniques for capturing physical models and computer aided inspection (CMM/scanners).
  • Cloud Manufacturing: Distributed manufacturing models leveraging internet networks.
  • AI & ML: Artificial intelligence and machine learning applications in modern CAD/CAM processes.

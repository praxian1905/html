Question A

(a) What are Internal Combustion Engines?

An Internal Combustion (IC) Engine is a thermal prime mover in which the chemical energy contained within a fuel is released via combustion inside the boundaries of a closed working cylinder.

✓ Energy Conversion Chain

The high-temperature, high-pressure combustion gases expand directly against mechanical engine parts (such as a piston or turbine blades), converting the liberated thermal energy directly into mechanical work.

Question B

(b) What are the classifications of IC Engines?

IC Engines are classified into distinct groups depending on structural and thermodynamic features. Key classification types include:

BY CYCLE / STROKE

Operating Cycle

Divided into Two-Stroke and Four-Stroke engines; or by thermodynamics into Otto, Diesel, and Dual cycles.

BY IGNITION MODE ★

Ignition Mechanism

Classified as Spark Ignition (SI) engines (petrol) or Compression Ignition (CI) engines (diesel).

BY FUEL TYPE

Fuel Consumed

Categorized by fuel types, including Petrol, Diesel, CNG / LPG, or Bi biofuels.

BY COOLING METHOD

Thermal Management

Separated into mechanical Air-cooled systems or closed Liquid-cooled jacketed configurations.

Question C

(c) What is the function of Fuel Injection Pump system?

In Compression Ignition (CI) engines, the Fuel Injection Pump (FIP) serves two primary high-precision operating functions:

FUNCTION 01

Pressure Elevation

It elevates the fuel pressure to extreme levels (often 200 to 2000+ bar) to overcome high in-cylinder air pressures and ensure complete fuel atomization.

FUNCTION 02

Metering & Timing

It measures the exact volume of fuel required based on engine load and delivers it at a precise crank angle moment.

Question D

(d) What is the Ignition lag?

Ignition lag (or ignition delay) is the precise time interval between the point where the initialization criteria are met and the actual measurable rise in cylinder pressure due to chemical combustion.

Ignition Lag (SI) = Moment of Flame Nucleus Growth - Moment of Spark Discharge Ignition Lag (CI) = Moment of Auto-Ignition Start - Moment of Injection Commencement

It consists of a physical delay period (atomization and mixing) and a chemical delay period (pre-flame oxidation reactions).

Question E

(e) What is Battery Ignition System?

A Battery Ignition System is an electrical assembly used in SI engines where a low-voltage storage battery supplies the primary electrical current needed to generate a high-voltage spark.

⚡ Principle of Induction

The system routes low-voltage current ($12V$) from the battery through the primary winding of an ignition coil. When a breaker point or electronic switch breaks this current, a high voltage ($20,000V–30,000V$) is induced in the secondary winding, which is then routed via a distributor to the appropriate spark plug.

Question F

(f) What is Brake Power?

Brake Power (BP) is the actual net useful mechanical power output available at the engine crankshaft for driving an external load.

Brake Power (BP) = Indicated Power (IP) - Frictional Power (FP) BP = (2 * π * N * T) / 60000 [kW] Where: N = Engine crankshaft rotational speed (RPM) T = Net output brake torque measured at dynamometer (N·m)

It is called "brake" power because it is traditionally measured using a mechanical brake mechanism or dynamometer applied to the output shaft.

Question G

(g) What is the need for Turbocharging system?

A Turbocharging system is an air forced-induction setup driven by the engine's exhaust gas energy. The primary reasons for turbocharging include:

Increased Power Density Downsizing Benefits Altitude Compensation
  • Enhanced Power Output: Compressing the intake air packs a higher mass of oxygen into the cylinders, allowing more fuel to be burned completely per stroke.
  • Engine Downsizing: Smaller, lighter engines can match the power output of larger naturally aspirated variants, improving overall vehicle fuel efficiency.
  • Altitude Compensation: At high elevations where air is thin, a turbocharger restores air density to prevent engine power drop-off.
Question H

(h) What is Air Cooling system?

An Air Cooling system is a thermal management design where waste heat is dissipated directly from the engine components to the surrounding atmosphere by convection.

✓ Extended Fin Surfaces

The cylinder barrels and cylinder heads are cast with thin, extended metallic paths known as cooling fins. These fins increase the overall conductive surface area, allowing fast convective heat transfer to the air currents passing around the engine block.

Question I

(i) What is the composition in CNG?

Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) is an eco-friendly alternative fuel composed primarily of light, volatile gaseous hydrocarbons.

Constituent Gas Compound Chemical Formula Approximate Percentage Volume
Methane (Primary) $CH_4$ 80% – 95%
Ethane $C_2H_6$ 2% – 5%
Propane & Butane traces $C_3H_8$ / $C_4H_{10}$ < 1% – 2%
Inert Gases (Nitrogen, $CO_2$) $N_2$ / $CO_2$ 1% – 3%
Question J

(j) When EURO standards started and where?

The EURO emission standards are unified regulatory criteria defining the acceptable limits for exhaust emissions from new vehicles sold across Europe.

TIMELINE ARTIFACT

Inception Year

The regulatory framework officially started in the year 1992 with the introduction of the EURO 1 standard tier.

GEOGRAPHIC SOURCE

Origin Region

The standards were formulated and enacted within the member states of the European Union (EU).
